## The problem

Keyword search missed anything phrased differently from the query. Pure vector search
found "related" documents but buried the exact match the user actually wanted. Neither
alone was good enough to ship.

## What I built

A hybrid pipeline that combines the strengths of both:

1. **Candidate generation** — BM25 for lexical precision, dense embeddings for semantic
   recall, fused with reciprocal-rank fusion.
2. **Cross-encoder reranking** — a fine-tuned reranker reorders the top candidates for
   relevance the bi-encoder can't capture.
3. **Serving** — `pgvector` for storage, Redis for hot-path caching, batched inference to
   keep tail latency flat under load.

## Outcome

On our internal eval set, hybrid + rerank beat keyword search by **+31% nDCG@10** while
holding **p95 latency under 90ms** at production traffic. Click-through on the top result
rose measurably within the first week.
