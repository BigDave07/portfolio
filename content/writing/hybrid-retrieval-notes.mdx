---
title: Notes on Hybrid Retrieval
summary: Why neither keyword nor vector search wins alone, and how to fuse them without wrecking your latency budget.
date: "2025-09-03"
---

Pure vector search is seductive and slightly wrong. It finds documents that are *about*
the same thing, then buries the exact answer the user typed. Keyword search has the
opposite failure. The fix is not to pick a side.

## Fuse candidates, then rerank

Generate candidates from both a lexical retriever (BM25) and a dense retriever, fuse them
with reciprocal-rank fusion, then let a cross-encoder reranker make the final call on the
top slice. Each stage covers the previous stage's blind spot.

## Watch the tail, not the mean

Rerankers are where latency goes to die. Batch inference, cap the rerank window, and cache
aggressively on the hot path. Optimize p95, because that's what users feel.

## Measure with your own eval set

Public benchmarks won't match your data. Build a small, honest eval set from real queries
and track nDCG@10 on every change. If the number doesn't move, neither should the code.
