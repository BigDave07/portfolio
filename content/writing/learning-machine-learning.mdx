I recently finished a series of IBM certifications covering Python for Data Science, SQL,
machine learning, deep learning, and unsupervised learning. Here are a few things that
stuck with me.

## Start with the data, not the model

The most valuable habit I picked up was spending real time on exploratory data analysis
before reaching for a model. Understanding distributions and relationships first made
every later step easier.

## Simple baselines earn their keep

A simple, well-understood model is a great baseline — and often surprisingly hard to beat.
